Cognitive ability was significantly associated with how well participants processed speech in noisy environments. Study included people with autism, fetal alcohol syndrome and a "neurotypical" control group. Lead investigator Bonnie Lau: "The relationship between cognitive ability and speech-perception performance transcended diagnostic categories" The authors suggested that neurodivergent individuals and individuals with lower cognitive ability could benefit from an assessment of the environments may challenge their hearing thresholds..
