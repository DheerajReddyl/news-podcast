Our next story is titled: Microsoft offers no-cost Windows 10 lifeline - Tech Xplore. Microsoft announced new options for US and European customers to safely extend the life of the Windows 10 operating system free of charge. The US tech giant plans to end support for Windows 10 on October 14. The move has drawn criticism from consumer advocacy groups and sparked concerns among users who fear they'll need to purchase new computers to stay protected from cyber threats. An estimated 650 million people worldwide were still using Windows 10 as of August, according to US-based Consumer Reports. The company had offered a one-year extension of Windows 10 security updates for $30 to users whose hardware is incompatible with Windows 11. And that concludes this report.
